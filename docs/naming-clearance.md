# Product-name clearance

Date: 2026-07-12.

Status: preliminary technical and common-law knockout search, not a legal opinion or a trademark
clearance opinion.

## Decision

**No screened name is ready to freeze.**

Do not begin E0 with the current **Assure** identity. Assure is already occupied across software
products and major package registries, is nearly impossible to search, and overstates what the
scanner establishes. Do not replace it with **DocWake** either: although the exact compound is
technically clean and its impact metaphor is unusually honest, the final United States trademark
knockout found active **DOCSWAVE** software services close enough in sight, sound, purchasers, and
commercial field to make adoption uncomfortable without counsel.

**DocsMaybe** produced the cleanest United States federal knockout result, but it remains a poor
default for a required enterprise control and has a live Maybe\* software neighbor in common-law
search. **ProseDeps** was the next-cleanest federal result, but active OpenProse already owns the
same Markdown/dependency/developer territory. Neither should be forced into service merely because
the exact package strings are free.

The next naming round must start with genuinely coined marks whose connection to impact or review
is indirect. Recombining functional English nouns repeatedly produced either a dominant-component
software mark, a close whole-mark collision, or a merely descriptive name. Once a new mark clears
common-law, package/domain, and professional trademark review, freeze it and migrate the dossier
before implementation or publication makes the wire strings compatibility commitments.

## What was checked

Negative search results are observations as of the date above, not reservations. Package names,
accounts, and domains can be taken at any time.

### Near-name and trademark risk

The decisive concern is active **DOCSWAVE**, registered for overlapping class-42 services including
software development, website development, and security-software work. `DOCWAKE` and `DOCSWAVE`
share the `DOC` opening, `WA` transition, terminal `E`, rhythm, and software purchasers; the
one-syllable difference is not a risk worth accepting casually. Active **DOCUWARE**
document-management and workflow registrations add congestion around the same commercial field.
See the official USPTO records for [DOCSWAVE](https://tsdr.uspto.gov/#caseNumber=79272070&caseSearchType=US_APPLICATION&caseType=DEFAULT&searchType=statusSearch)
and [DOCUWARE](https://tsdr.uspto.gov/#caseNumber=79252171&caseSearchType=US_APPLICATION&caseType=DEFAULT&searchType=statusSearch).

There is also meaningful `Wake` congestion in the same developer audience:

- [SiFive Wake](https://github.com/sifiveinc/wake) is an active build-orchestration language and
  dependency-analysis tool, with a [VS Code
  extension](https://marketplace.visualstudio.com/items?itemName=sifive.wake).
- [Ackee Wake](https://ackee.xyz/wake/docs/latest/) is a Solidity development, testing, fuzzing,
  and static-analysis framework with a `wake` command and GitHub Actions.
- [`wake-build`](https://docs.rs/crate/wake-build/latest) and a newer [universal task-runner
  Wake](https://pkg.go.dev/github.com/vivalchemy/wake) add command and search noise.

No evidence was found that those projects use **DocWake**, but both SiFive Wake and the proposed
tool analyze dependency/change relationships in developer workflows. Combined with DOCSWAVE, the
name is a hold rather than a working recommendation. Counsel would need to search live and dead
word marks containing `WAKE`, `DOC WAKE`, `DOCS WAVE`, and phonetic equivalents in classes 9 and
42, together with relevant unregistered uses, before reconsidering it.

The WIPO Global Brand Database is a useful knockout source, but [WIPO explicitly recommends also
searching national and regional registers](https://www.wipo.int/en/web/global-brand-database/faqs_branddb)
and does not treat an empty result as an availability opinion. This investigation is not a
substitute for that work.

### Product and language fit

The name passes the core truthfulness test:

- a code change can leave a set of structurally connected documents in its *wake*;
- the scanner can identify that set and definite broken references;
- the name does not claim that impacted prose is stale, false, reviewed, or automatically fixed;
- the metaphor still fits future typed cross-artifact relations and impact traversal.

The intended surfaces read naturally:

```text
docwake check
.docwake/scanner-policy.json
docwake:v1/reference
DocWake found two broken references and eight impacted documents.
```

`DocWake` is short, pronounceable, and easy to type. Its small language weaknesses are that speech
can sound like “dock wake,” and *wake* can mean awakening, a ship's trail, or a funeral gathering.
The tagline anchors the intended ship/impact metaphor, but strong product fit does not cure the
legal concern.

A last bounded round deliberately avoided the exhausted `doc`, `drift`, `sync`, `invariant`,
`prose`, `wake`, `radius`, `concord`, `tether`, `trace`, and `maybe` families. It still produced no
responsible winner:

The official records supporting the closest recovery-round conflicts are
[IMPACTVUE](https://tsdr.uspto.gov/#caseNumber=97978958&caseSearchType=US_APPLICATION&caseType=DEFAULT&searchType=statusSearch),
[CUE](https://tsdr.uspto.gov/#caseNumber=79391777&caseSearchType=US_APPLICATION&caseType=DEFAULT&searchType=statusSearch),

The trademark pass used current USPTO exact, close-wordmark, and class-9/42 knockout searches. The
USPTO evaluates sound, appearance, meaning, commercial impression, and related goods/services—not
edit distance or Nice class alone. Its [likelihood-of-confusion
guidance](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/search/likelihood-confusion) and [comprehensive-search
guidance](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/search/comprehensive-clearance-search-similar-trademarks)
are the controlling caveats. WIPO, EUIPO/TMview, and UKIPO interactive negative searches could not
be completed defensibly through their CAPTCHA/WAF-protected interfaces and remain mandatory manual
or counsel checks for any future candidate.

## Rename gate before E0

`assure` is not merely prose in the current dossier. A case-insensitive scan found 608 matching
namespace/name occurrences across 46 files. They include:

- the CLI and human output;
- `.assure/` policy and future state paths;
- Markdown reference labels and the `assure:` URI scheme;
- JSON Schema IDs and URNs;
- hash-domain separators and canonical preimages;
- action/workflow examples;
- fixtures, canonical reports, validators, and expected digest vectors.

A mechanical global replacement is unsafe. Hash-domain changes intentionally alter every dependent
digest, historical discussion sometimes needs to retain the old spelling, and all schemas,
examples, scripts, and prose contracts must move together. Once a future coined mark receives the
user's name decision and professional clearance, perform one dedicated pre-E0 migration that:

1. freezes one brand and one lowercase public machine spelling;
2. replaces every active CLI, path, scheme, schema, and domain-separation identifier;
3. regenerates all canonical JSON and digest vectors from their sources rather than editing hashes;
4. preserves explicitly historical rejected syntax only where the history is still useful;
5. reruns every dossier validator and checks that no active `assure` namespace remains;
6. reserves the domains and service handles through normal registrar/provider checkout immediately
   before announcing the name.

No rename or implementation is authorized by this report. Naming remains blocked; the current
`assure` strings are research placeholders, not an acceptable public identity.
