# Roadmap

The local command is the whole product today, and the boundaries it states are the roadmap's
table of contents.

The nearest lane is provider-verified controls. The request wire, a signed envelope carrying
the evaluation request and the five external controls, is specified in the dossier's RFC and
deliberately unbuilt; the report format already carries its fields as `none`. When it lands,
an organization floor, adoption debt, waivers, trusted time, and execution constraints become
usable in required checks, with every control identity-bound to the exact tree it authorizes.
The `amiss-bootstrap` wrapper and the release manifest chain, which validate a pinned action
tree and launch a verified engine, ship in the repository now and wait on the same lane.

Reference semantics grow only where a boundary can become exact. Anchor and heading-slug
resolution is the most requested candidate, and it enters only with a slug algorithm pinned
the way the parsers are pinned, because a wrong guess about a slugger is a false pass.
reStructuredText and AsciiDoc adapters fit the same slot the MDX adapter occupies today:
pinned grammar, conformance corpus, honest opacity for what the grammar cannot see.

The governed layer, typed claims, acceptance seals, review verification, everything the
rejected predecessor wanted to be, stays closed behind the dossier's gates. The contracts
exist, the identity and lifecycle semantics are worked out, and none of it is authorized
until the scanner has earned adoption evidence and the state-layer experiments pass. The
project's position is unchanged: a check that cannot yet be made trustworthy is not shipped
in a trustworthy costume.

Performance work follows measurement. The contract ceilings bound the worst case; the
benchmark lane exists to keep the common case honest, and known quadratic corners in the
pinned upstream grammars are documented in the corpus notes rather than patched around.
