# Naming candidates, round two

Date: 2026-07-12.

Status: preliminary knockout screening, same standing as [naming-clearance.md](./naming-clearance.md):
point-in-time observations, not reservations, not a trademark clearance opinion. Every candidate
below still requires professional USPTO/EUIPO/UKIPO search and registrar checkout before freeze.
This round honored the clearance file's constraints: no name from the exhausted families (doc,
drift, sync, invariant, prose, wake, radius, concord, tether, trace, maybe, cue, witness, tally,
impact), and no recombined functional nouns. The tension between "genuinely coined" and the
owner's "clear what it is about" was resolved with arbitrary dictionary words from remote craft
domains (textual criticism, surveying, textiles, hydrology): legally strong the way arbitrary
marks are, and each carrying a one-sentence story that lands.

Method: one generation agent produced 28 candidates under the constraints; twelve names from
unexhausted metaphor families plus its five best novel ones went through a full knockout screen
(GitHub repos, orgs, and users; LinkedIn company search, best effort through search snippets; exact
probes of crates.io, npm, PyPI, RubyGems; RDAP for .com and .dev; trademark signal through public
secondary sources; slang and meaning scan across Spanish, French, German, Portuguese, Italian,
Japanese romaji; CLI ergonomics). Forty-nine names were deep-screened across six waves; waves
three through six, run after the owner raised the bar to "better than Freshet or Corrigenda", are
at the bottom of this file. After the owner rejected the Latin register ("moderner name, not latin
stuff"), wave six targeted modern dev-tool naming and produced the campaign's only outright clean
verdict, Klopt, plus a second likely-clean, Amiss. The standing modern-register slate: Klopt,
Amiss, Tarkka, with Trueup as a conditional hold; the Latin-register survivors (Lustrum, Veedor,
Vidimus, Corrigenda, Siglum) move to the bench by owner taste, not by clearance.

### Siglum

In a scholarly critical apparatus, a siglum is the symbol that stands for a source manuscript;
sigla are documented references to sources, which is this tool's subject matter exactly. Of every
name screened in both rounds, it is the most literal fit for a reference checker while remaining
an arbitrary mark in software.

Screen results: crates.io, npm, PyPI, and RubyGems all returned 404, the only wave-two candidate
with a clean sweep. The GitHub org [Siglum](https://github.com/Siglum) is parked with zero repos
(2023). The only exact-name project is
[SiglumProject/siglum](https://github.com/SiglumProject/siglum), a 5-star WebAssembly LaTeX
compiler, alive as of May 2026. The only exact-name company found is Siglum Technologies LLC
(Texas, 2015, small, liveness unclear); no LinkedIn company page surfaced. siglum.com has been
registered since 2010 but does not resolve (dormant hold); siglum.dev was registered on
2026-07-07, five days before this screen, still in add-grace, registrant unknown and plausibly the
LaTeX project. No trademark signal surfaced. Italian and Spanish "sigla" is a benign cognate that
reinforces the meaning.

Risks: the spoken neighborhood is crowded ([Sigil](https://github.com/Sigil-Ebook/Sigil), the
6,900-star EPUB editor, plus sigilum and "Siglume" projects in AI tooling), so "did you say
Sigil?" will happen; the learned plural is a prose quirk. If chosen, the first action is
identifying the siglum.dev registrant immediately; that registration is days old and the string is
visibly being discovered.

### Freshet

A freshet is the rush of fresh water when the snow melts: freshness flushing through stale places.
Two syllables, vivid, and the metaphor explains the product's job in one sentence.

Screen results: no live conflict of substance anywhere. The largest bearer is a stream-processing
research project abandoned in 2016-17
([FreshetDMS](https://github.com/FreshetDMS/Freshet-Deprecated), 46 stars) and the Freshet
Collective, a Standing Rock legal-defense nonprofit in a different universe. No company, no
product, no trademark hit (nearest marks are FRESHLETES and FRESHR). The catch is operational:
all four exact package names are held by unrelated micro-projects (a 16-download Solana crate from
June 2026, a "for testing purposes" npm 0.0.1, a small PyPI caching tool, and a literal "reserved"
RubyGems squat), freshet.com is parked since 2004, and freshet.dev was registered 2026-03-21 by
someone else.

Risks: launch happens on qualified handles (freshet-ci, getfreshet.com) or after negotiating or
disputing the squats, several of which look contestable. The word saw three independent pickups in
2024-2026, so it will not stay quiet. Adjacent embarrassment is minor but real: "Freshette" is a
female urination funnel brand one letter away.

### Resurvey

To resurvey is to measure the territory again and correct the official map, which is the dossier's
own map-versus-territory framing turned into a verb. The most self-explanatory name in the field:
no story required at all.

Screen results: the only round-one candidate with a full clean sweep: crates.io, npm, PyPI, and
RubyGems all 404, resurvey.dev showing no RDAP object, no product, no company (the GitHub org
[resurvey](https://github.com/resurvey) is an empty shell), no LinkedIn page, and zero trademark
hits. resurvey.com is parked since 2002, acquirability unknown. The word's public usage is land
administration and ecology science, none of it branded software.

Risks: it is a generic dictionary verb, which makes it a weak mark to own and dilutes search with
land-records and ecology results; some users will hyphenate "re-survey"; it reads as a verb rather
than a brand, and `resurvey check` has a slight semantic stutter since resurveying already means
checking. The cleanest claim, the plainest name.

### Corrigenda

A corrigenda is the published list of things to be corrected, which is literally what the tool
emits. Scholars and publishers have used the word for centuries; nobody in software owns it.

Screen results: crates.io, npm, PyPI, and RubyGems all 404, and corrigenda.dev shows no RDAP
object. The only live commercial user is Corrigenda Limited, a UK facilities-management (HVAC)
firm acquired by Apleona in 2025, about as far-field as collisions get. The GitHub handle belongs
to an individual game developer; the only exact-name repo is a zero-star dormant dataset. No
trademark signal. Portuguese uses the word natively for a corrections list; nothing embarrassing
in any scanned language.

Risks: ergonomics. Four syllables, ten letters, and the corrigendum/corrigenda plural trap
guarantees misspellings ("corriganda"). `corrigenda check` is a mouthful, though the bare command
emitting your corrections list reads well. Search results are flooded with academic errata notices
and government tender corrections.

## Round three: eight names against a raised bar

Bar: beat Freshet's squatted namespace and Corrigenda's ergonomics at the same time. Eight names
from four previously unmined domains (legal fact-finding Latin, assay metallurgy, calibration
standards, manuscript lineage) went through the full knockout. Five died, three hold, and one of
the holds is the best story any round has produced.

### Constat: the best name found, and lost in a thirteen-hour window

Latin "it is established"; in French law a constat is a bailiff's certified report of observed
facts, no conclusions drawn, and constater means "to establish by observation" across French,
Spanish, Portuguese, and Italian. That is this tool's own spec language (observation facts, never
judgments) as a single two-syllable word. `constat check` reads cleanly; npm and RubyGems are
free; the only crates.io occupant is a dead 2020 toy routable with `constat-cli`; no trademark
signal at all; the French bailiff-app market (EasyConstat, i-Constat) uses compound names, and the
nearest company is a regional Brazilian IT-services firm.

The gate: PyPI `constat` (0.0.1, "verifiable evidence infrastructure for investigations") and
constat.dev were both claimed on 2026-07-11, one day before this screen, by an unknown party whose
homepage is a GoDaddy waitlist page and whose declared repo is private or deleted. That is a
same-week land grab in an adjacent space, not a shipped product. Action: re-probe the claimant in
four to six weeks; if it is vapor, Constat upgrades to the top of the whole slate; if it ships,
Constat is out.

Re-checked 2026-07-12, decision-grade, and the window closed. There are two independent claimants,
thirteen hours apart on 2026-07-11. Actor A (theconstat.com 06:06 UTC, PyPI 17:11) is a solo
reservation: the wheel is 1.4 KB, the description says "Reserved package name", the linked repo is
private or deleted, and the account's public life is an emulator fork. Actor B (constat.dev 18:45,
getconstat.com 19:00) is the real one: between the two screens the site went live as a finished
product, "Constat: FDA Device Evidence, Established from the Record", operated by Health AI (two
NYU PhDs, company founded 2019), with a public MCP endpoint that Claude can connect to and terms
dated 2026-07-11. Three products now pitch "constat = certified observed facts" on the exact
string, one of them shipped, developer-facing, and holding the .dev a CI tool would want. Under
the DOCSWAVE precedent (a sound-alike in an overlapping field was disqualifying), an exact-string
twin in evidence tooling is strictly worse. Verdict: closed for a standalone identity. The only
defensible residue is a scoped open-source plugin handle (`constat-ci` is free on PyPI, GitHub,
and constat-ci.com) with no brand ambition, no paid offering, and a professional FR/EU search
first; PEP 541 against a one-day-old intentional claim is a dead end, and npm plus RubyGems being
free does not buy back `pip install constat` or the .dev.

### Assize: claimable today, with one fight and one homophone

The Assize of Bread and Ale was the standing official inspection that verified weights and
measures, which is the product's job description with seven centuries of provenance. It actually
clears the raised bar on both axes: crates, npm, RubyGems, and assize.dev are all free (a better
namespace than Freshet ever had), and two easy syllables beat Corrigenda's four. GitHub is nearly
empty; no company, no trademark signal.

Costs, stated plainly: PyPI `assize` is a 0.0.0 placeholder reserved 2026-06-15 with the summary
"Reserved - independent verification for spec-driven development", meaning a rival in this exact
space reserved it four weeks ago (contest via PEP 541, which is slow and discretionary, or ship as
`assize-ci`); spoken, `assize check` is a perfect homophone of "a size check"; the French hear
their serious-crimes court (cour d'assises); and the first three letters carry a mild snicker tax,
shared with assay and assess, that content filters in practice do not trip on.

### Apograph: the distinctive fallback

An apograph is the copy made from an original and checked against the autograph, which is the
docs-versus-code relation in one scholarly word. Crates, npm, RubyGems, and apograph.dev are free;
GitHub search is near-silent; every language scan came back benign; three syllables say and type
cleanly. The wounds: PyPI belongs to a live, unrelated HTML-to-PPTX tool (April 2026, not
reclaimable as a squat), the .com and LinkedIn slug belong to a UK managed-IT firm founded 1993,
and a dormant blockchain platform uses the Apograf spelling one letter away. Viable if pip is not
the primary distribution channel.

## Round four: certification words and coinages

Same raised bar. Eight names from medieval certification vocabulary, inspection idioms, and
controlled coinages. Two clear the bar, one is a story-perfect hold, five die.

### Konstat: the cleanest claimable namespace of the campaign

A coined K-spelling drawing on Swedish konstatera, German konstatieren, and French constater, all
meaning "to establish as fact by observation", which is the scanner's spec language verbatim. All
four package registries and konstat.dev are free; no company anywhere in software uses the word;
no trademark signal exists; two syllables, no plural trap, and `konstat check` is the crispest CLI
line screened in any wave. Because the bare form is a coinage in every language that has the verb,
it is legally the strongest kind of mark on the slate.

The dings, stated plainly: github.com/konstat case-folds onto KonstaT, a prominent and active
Android developer, so the org ships as konstat-dev or similar; konstat.com is a Mexican family
genealogy site renewed to 2031 (non-competing, not obtainable); the "-stat" ending can misread as
a vmstat-family metrics tool; and it is a spoken homophone of Constat. That last ding hardened
into a demotion on 2026-07-12: the Constat re-check found Health AI's Constat shipped at
constat.dev as a live evidence product with an MCP endpoint, so Konstat would forever be one
spoken letter from an active product in adjacent tooling. By the DOCSWAVE sound-alike precedent,
Konstat is now a hold, not a finalist, pending counsel's view on coexistence.

### Lustrum: the only full-slate real word left

The lustrum was Rome's five-yearly purification and census: the periodic re-verification of the
entire citizen roll, which is what a docs-versus-code re-check does on every run. All four package
registries are free, the only such real-word slate found in four waves. No software product, no
trademark hit, no live company in class 9/42 territory; the GitHub user is a one-repo account
dormant since 2016. Romance languages all carry the five-year sense natively (lustro, "depuis des
lustres"), and the word's public life is Dutch student-association anniversary galas, which is
benign noise. Two syllables, regular spelling, `lustrum check` reads cleanly.

The dings: lustrum.com was grabbed by a domain speculator in 2024 and is parked for sale
(buyable at aftermarket price); lustrum.dev was registered 2026-03-30 by an unknown party, fifteen
weeks old and inside the land-grab window, the one item to identify before committing; the "lust"
opening carries a mild snicker tax that the Lustre filesystem has survived in professional
settings for two decades; and classical Latin has an obscure homograph (a beast's den, and worse)
that is Cicero-commentary trivia a Hacker News classicist will mention exactly once.

### Vidimus: the story-perfect hold

Latin "we have seen": a vidimus is the medieval certified copy, so called because the certifying
authority wrote vidimus on it after inspection. As a name for a tool whose acceptance gesture is
"a human looked, still fine", nothing screened comes close semantically, and all four registries
plus vidimus.dev are free today. Held, not advanced: kabNath/vidimus is a live eight-week-old
exact-name project building Ed25519-signed attestations for AI evaluation (the same conceptual
space), pushed nine days before the screen, and it will take PyPI at its first release; a second
exact-name repo appeared two days before the screen. Re-probe before any commitment; if the
project stalls at zero stars, this name reopens.

## Round five: other traditions and deep English

Four waves of Western-European dictionary words met accelerating land grabs, so this wave shifted
stock: inspector words from Spanish, Turkish, Swahili, and Finnish tradition, plus deep-English
fidelity words. Eight screened; two clear the bar, one holds, five die, and one of the deaths is

### Veedor: the best claimability result of all forty-one names

The veedor was the Spanish crown's official inspector, and the word still works: Latin-American
veedurías are civic oversight bodies, and in Jerez the veedor certifies grape quality at the
winery door. Uniquely in five waves, everything is free at once: all four package registries, the
GitHub handle itself (no case-fold problem, no qualified org needed), and veedor.dev. Spanish
speakers get the meaning instantly, Italians hear "vedo" (I see), and the -dor agent ending reads
inspector-ish even to English monolinguals through the matador pattern. Two syllables,
`veedor check` reads cleanly.

Dings: veedor.com was drop-caught in December 2024 and is parked for sale at aftermarket price; an
unknown "Veedor Solutions" holds the LinkedIn company slug; the double-e invites "vedor" typos
from people who only hear it; and VeedorIA, a Dominican procurement-monitoring project created
eighteen days before the screen, shows the oversight crowd circling the word.

### Tarkka: the precise word

Finnish for precise, exact, careful, which is the product promise as an adjective. All four
registries and tarkka.dev are free; exactly one exact-name repository exists on all of GitHub (a
1-star dictionary converter); no trademark hits at all; no dev-tool collision anywhere. The
occupants of the word are a Boston machining-education duo, a Finnish grocery app, a satellite
service by the Finnish Environment Institute, and surname businesses (it is a common Finnish
surname). Two crisp syllables; `tarkka check` almost sounds like the gate snapping shut.

Dings: the double k is a real spelling trap (English hands type "tarka", so the docs own the
misspelling and alias it); the bare GitHub username is a dormant 2013 squat, so the org ships as
tarkka-dev or similar; and as a dictionary adjective it is a weaker mark to defend than a coinage,
though the class 9/42 field is empty today.

### Hakiki: claimable but never ownable

Turkish for genuine, and the Swahili verb for verify; Tanzania's tax authority literally ships a
stamp-verification app called Hakiki Stempu. Every registry and the GitHub handle are free, and
the semantics are a bullseye twice over. Held anyway: "hakiki deri" (genuine leather) is an entire
Turkish commerce vertical, and Hakiki is a common Indonesian personal name, so exact-name search
belongs to leather wallets and other people forever. A name you can claim but never own.

## Round six: the modern register

The owner rejected the Latin/scholarly register; the target became modern dev-tool naming (the
Bun, Vite, Knip, Sentry shelf): short, crisp, contemporary, with living-language one-liners
explicitly in bounds since Knip is Dutch for "snip". Eight names screened; one clean, one
likely-clean, one hold, five rejects.

### Klopt: the campaign's first clean verdict

Dutch "dat klopt": "that's right, it checks out"; "klopt niet" is the natural failure message, so
the tool's own pass and fail states are the name. One syllable, five letters. As of 2026-07-12,
all four package registries and klopt.dev are unclaimed, no product or company anywhere uses the
bare word (only Dutch consultancies with longer names like Buro 't Klopt), the largest exact-name
repository has one star and died in 2021, and klopt.com is a parked Dutch domain. No trademark
signal in any reachable register. The vulgarity check came back clear: "klopt" is what a
fact-checker says on television, and the nearest ugly word shares only two letters. German
neighbors "klopft" (knocks), benign and even apt.

Residue: the github.com/klopt username is a dormant two-repo account from 2022, so the org ships
as klopt-dev or via GitHub's inactive-username process; people who have only heard the word may
type "clopt" once; and the one remaining diligence item is a BOIP/EUIPO exact-word search, since
the word is Dutch. `klopt check` reads clean, and `klopt` alone printing green is the brand.

### Amiss: the wry English option

"Something is amiss" is instantly understood, and a problem-finder wearing the word sits in the
same school as Sentry and lint: named for the bad news it delivers. All four registries are free,
npm noise is ten packages (against 467 for skew and 1,270 for intact), no company of consequence
uses the word, and no trademark signal surfaced. Collisions in full: a dormant zero-repo GitHub
handle, an 8-star bioinformatics repo, and a two-person consultancy that registered amiss.dev on
2024-09-18, which is the one item to watch since both obvious domains (amiss.com held since 2002,
amiss.dev live) need a workaround like getamiss.com or amiss.sh. Spoken, "amiss check" can land as
"a miss check", which reads as intentional wordplay more than embarrassment.

The voice argument, recorded for the decision: the output writes itself in natural English. The
failure line is "3 things amiss in docs/cli.mdx"; the success line is "Nothing amiss.", which
doubles as the tagline; "amiss" contains "a miss", the update that didn't happen. It is a
failure-state name where Klopt is a pass-state name, so its green message carries a negation, and
English idiom's "run amiss" (to go wrong) is either a wink or a jinx depending on the reader. The
name claims only that the tool notices when something is off, which matches the spec's
observation-not-judgment epistemics exactly.

### Trueup: hold, claim-now if pursued

Accounting's "true up" is the exact semantics (reconcile the recorded against the actual), and all
four registries were open on screen day. Held anyway: trueup.io is a famous live brand aimed at
the same audience (every tech worker who watched the layoff cycle knows its tracker), TruUp is a
phonetic twin selling billing reconciliation, the word's orthography drifts four ways (true up,
true-up, TrueUp, TruUp), and four exact-name GitHub repos appeared between 2025-12-28 and
2026-05-02. If chosen despite that, the registries get claimed the same day.
